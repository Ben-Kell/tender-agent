## Background

DICT required a comprehensive digital transformation of its service-delivery platforms to modernise citizen-facing services, improve internal productivity, and reduce the total cost of ownership of its legacy ICT estate. The programme was delivered under tight budget constraints and against a challenging timeline driven by incoming legislative requirements.

## Our Approach

We partnered with DICT to design and deliver a phased transformation programme, combining platform modernisation with process reengineering and change management:

- **Legacy Application Migration** – Migrated [X] legacy applications from on-premise data centres to a compliant cloud environment (ASD-certified cloud provider), reducing infrastructure costs by [X]%.
- **Digital Service Platform** – Delivered a new citizen-facing service portal based on the Australian Government's Digital Service Standard, including accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1 AA) and mobile-responsive design.
- **Data & Analytics Uplift** – Implemented a modern data platform enabling near-real-time reporting and analytics, replacing manual spreadsheet-based reporting processes.
- **Change Management** – Delivered a structured change-management and training programme across [X] business units, achieving [X]% staff adoption within 3 months of go-live.
- **Service Desk Transition** – Transitioned DICT's service desk to our managed service model, reducing average resolution time by [X]% within the first six months.

## Transition Methodology

The programme used our proven phased transition approach:

1. Discovery and current-state assessment (6 weeks).
2. Platform design and stakeholder co-design workshops (8 weeks).
3. Development, testing, and user-acceptance testing (16 weeks).
4. Phased rollout by business unit with hypercare support (12 weeks).
5. Business-as-usual handover and lessons-learned review (4 weeks).
