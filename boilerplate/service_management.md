# Service Management

## Overview

We deliver services using a structured service management framework aligned with ITIL 4 principles and tailored to Australian Government requirements. Our approach ensures consistent, measurable service delivery with clear accountability at every level.

## Service Management Framework

Our framework is built on four pillars:

1. **Service Design** – Services are designed with the end-user and Commonwealth outcomes in mind, with explicit service definitions, SLAs, and OLAs agreed prior to commencement.
2. **Service Transition** – Structured handover processes ensure continuity and knowledge retention when services move between providers or phases.
3. **Service Operation** – Day-to-day operations are governed by documented procedures, supported by a certified ITSM toolset, and monitored against agreed performance targets.
4. **Continual Service Improvement** – Regular service reviews, trend analysis, and improvement registers drive ongoing enhancement of service quality and efficiency.

## Service Desk & Support Model

| Support Tier | Description | Coverage |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Tier 1 | Service desk – incident logging, triage, and first-contact resolution | Business hours / 24×7 (as contracted) |
| Tier 2 | Technical support – in-depth troubleshooting and resolution | Business hours / on-call |
| Tier 3 | Specialist / vendor escalation | As required |

## Service Levels

We propose the following standard service levels, which are negotiable based on Commonwealth requirements:

| Priority | Definition | Response Time | Resolution Time |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| P1 – Critical | Total loss of service | 15 minutes | 4 hours |
| P2 – High | Significant degradation | 30 minutes | 8 hours |
| P3 – Medium | Partial loss / workaround available | 2 hours | 24 hours |
| P4 – Low | Minor issue / cosmetic | 4 hours | 5 business days |

## Reporting & Governance

- **Monthly Service Reports** – Performance against all SLAs, incident trends, capacity utilisation, and improvement actions.
- **Quarterly Service Reviews** – Formal governance meeting with Commonwealth stakeholders to review performance, priorities, and roadmap.
- **Incident & Problem Management** – Root cause analysis (RCA) for all P1/P2 incidents delivered within 5 business days.
- **Change Management** – All changes follow an approved change-management process with Commonwealth sign-off for major changes.

## Tooling

We operate an enterprise-grade ITSM platform (ServiceNow / equivalent) that provides:

- Real-time incident and request tracking with full audit trail.
- Self-service portal for end-users.
- Automated escalation and notification workflows.
- Integration with Commonwealth monitoring and reporting systems where required.

## Continuous Improvement

We maintain a formal Service Improvement Plan (SIP) and track improvement initiatives through a live register reviewed at each quarterly governance meeting. Improvement targets are agreed with the Commonwealth and reported against monthly.
