# Transition Approach

## Overview

[COMPANY NAME] employs a structured, low-risk transition methodology to ensure continuity of service during transition-in and, where applicable, transition-out.

## Transition-In

### Phase 1 – Mobilisation (Weeks 1–2)
- Establish transition governance and steering committee
- Agree transition plan, milestones, and success criteria with the agency
- Identify and on-board key personnel; confirm clearance requirements
- Initiate knowledge transfer sessions with incumbent provider (if applicable)

### Phase 2 – Knowledge Transfer (Weeks 3–6)
- Document existing services, configurations, and runbooks
- Shadow incumbent operations to validate understanding
- Stand up parallel tooling and monitoring environments

### Phase 3 – Parallel Run (Weeks 7–8)
- Run new environment in parallel with incumbent
- Validate service desk, monitoring, and reporting functions
- Confirm SLA baselines and escalation paths

### Phase 4 – Cutover and Stabilisation (Week 9+)
- Execute cutover to full [COMPANY NAME] service ownership
- 30-day stabilisation period with enhanced hypercare support
- Formal transition-in completion review with agency stakeholders

## Transition-Out

Should the contract conclude, [COMPANY NAME] is committed to an orderly transition to the agency or an incoming provider, including:

- Full documentation handover
- Knowledge transfer to incoming provider
- Data export and destruction in accordance with agency requirements
- Ongoing support during a mutually agreed exit period

## Risk Management

Key transition risks and mitigations are maintained in a live risk register, reviewed at each governance checkpoint.
