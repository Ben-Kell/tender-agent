# Transition Approach

## Overview

We recognise that transitions carry inherent risk to service continuity and staff. Our structured transition approach minimises disruption, ensures knowledge is captured and transferred effectively, and delivers the new operating model on time and within budget.

## Transition Principles

- **No-Surprise Transition** – Commonwealth stakeholders are informed at every step through regular communication and governance checkpoints.
- **Business Continuity First** – Service continuity is maintained throughout; transition activities are scheduled to avoid peak-demand periods.
- **Knowledge-Led Handover** – Comprehensive documentation and structured knowledge transfer sessions prevent loss of institutional knowledge.
- **Risk-Driven Planning** – Risks are identified early, owned, and mitigated; a dedicated transition risk register is maintained and reviewed weekly.

## Transition Phases

### Phase 1 – Mobilisation (Weeks 1–4)

| Activity | Owner | Output |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Establish transition governance and communication plan | Transition Manager | Governance framework document |
| Confirm key personnel and obtain required clearances | HR / Security Officer | Cleared personnel onboarded |
| Stand up transition programme office | Transition Manager | Programme office operational |
| Baseline current-state documentation review | Technical Lead | Gap analysis report |
| Agree transition schedule with Commonwealth | Transition Manager | Agreed transition plan |

### Phase 2 – Knowledge Transfer (Weeks 3–8)

| Activity | Owner | Output |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Shadow existing service team | Technical Lead | Shadowing log |
| Document as-is processes, configurations, and dependencies | Technical Lead | As-is documentation package |
| Identify and resolve documentation gaps | Technical Lead | Updated documentation |
| Conduct structured knowledge transfer workshops | Training Lead | Workshop attendance records |
| Validate understanding via test scenarios | Technical Lead | Validated knowledge base |

### Phase 3 – Parallel Operation (Weeks 7–10)

| Activity | Owner | Output |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Run parallel operations with existing provider | Transition Manager | Parallel-run report |
| Resolve open issues and knowledge gaps | Technical Lead | Issue closure register |
| Obtain Commonwealth sign-off on readiness | Transition Manager | Readiness sign-off |

### Phase 4 – Cutover & Stabilisation (Weeks 10–14)

| Activity | Owner | Output |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Execute cutover plan (agreed date/time) | Transition Manager | Cutover record |
| Hypercare support period (heightened monitoring and response) | Service Delivery Manager | Hypercare report |
| Transition lessons-learned review | Transition Manager | Lessons-learned register |
| Formal transition closure | Transition Manager | Transition closure certificate |

## Transition Governance

A Transition Steering Committee will meet fortnightly, comprising:

- Commonwealth Programme Manager (Chair)
- Our Transition Manager
- Technical Lead(s)
- Incumbent provider representative (where applicable)

Progress against the transition plan, risks, and issues will be reported at each meeting.

## Incumbent Collaboration

We will work constructively and professionally with any incumbent provider, honouring any contractual obligations around data handover, documentation, and knowledge transfer. We have extensive experience managing incumbent relationships to achieve smooth transitions without service disruption.
