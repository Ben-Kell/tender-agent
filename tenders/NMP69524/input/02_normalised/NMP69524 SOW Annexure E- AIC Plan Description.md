TITLE:	Australian Industry CAPABILITY PLAN

DESCRIPTION and intended use

The Australian Industry Capability (AIC) Plan sets out the Supplier’s strategy, plans, processes and responsibilities for achieving the AIC Objectives and satisfying the AIC Obligations, including describing the management of the AIC program for the Work Order Term.  To the maximum practicable extent, the AIC Plan is written to contain specific and measureable commitments by the Supplier.

The Supplier uses the AIC Plan to:

set out the strategies, plans, processes, responsibilities and timeframes for the delivery of the AIC program and achieving the AIC Objectives and satisfying the AIC Obligations;

manage, coordinate and monitor the delivery of the AIC program;

ensure that those parties (including AIC Subcontractors) who are undertaking AIC-related activities understand their responsibilities, the commitments to be achieved, and the time-frames involved;

integrate the AIC activities being performed by AIC Subcontractors with the Supplier’s AIC activities to ensure that a coherent and cohesive AIC program is realised; and

provide assurance to the Commonwealth that the Supplier’s plan for delivering the AIC program will enable the AIC Objectives and AIC Obligations to be achieved while satisfying the other requirements of the Work Order.

The Commonwealth uses the AIC Plan to:

evaluate and gain assurance that the Supplier’s AIC program will achieve the AIC requirements of the Work Order;

provide a basis for monitoring and assessing the Supplier’s performance in relation to the execution of the AIC program;

assess the Supplier’s willingness and ability to work collaboratively with the Commonwealth and Australian Industry to achieve the AIC Objectives and AIC Obligations; and

identify the Commonwealth’s involvement in the AIC program.

INTER-RELATIONSHIPS

PREPARATION INSTRUCTIONS

Generic Format and Content

The AIC Plan shall comply with the general format, content and preparation instructions contained in this document.

When the Work Order has specified delivery of another plan that contains aspects of the required information, the AIC Plan shall summarise these aspects and refer to the other plan.

The AIC Plan shall be written in accordance with the following requirements:

the Supplier’s commitments are to be separated from any background or descriptive information;

the background or descriptive information is to be no more than 25% of the overall size of the AIC Plan (measured by page count), and is to be clearly identified as background and/or descriptive material;

the Supplier’s commitments are to be set out using the following terminology as appropriate:

except when using one of the forms in paragraph (ii) below, the words, “the Supplier shall […]”, are to be used to set out absolute commitments;

the words, “the Supplier shall use its best endeavours to […]” or “the Supplier shall use its reasonable endeavours to […]”, are to be used when the Supplier is not able to make an absolute commitment, but a qualified commitment is required; and

the words, “the Supplier may […]”, are to be used when there is little-to-no certainty that a particular path or decision will actually occur or be made, or the matter is discretionary; and

any matters or assumptions which qualify the Supplier’s commitments are to be clearly stated individually in respect of each commitment, and the relevant commitment is to be expressed to be subject to those qualifications using specific cross references.  Any background or descriptive information is not to qualify the Supplier’s commitments.  Generic words, such as “where appropriate”, “when appropriate”, or “as required” are not to be used to qualify the Supplier’s commitments.

The AIC Plan is subject to Approval in accordance with the requirements of the Work Order and Statement of Work.  To allow for more objective assessment of the AIC Plan, Approval will be based upon the following criteria:

the program of AIC activities achieves the AIC Objectives and satisfies the AIC Obligations;

the overall Australian Contract Expenditure (ACE) for the Work Order and the Prescribed ACE Percentages are reasonable and achievable in the context of the Services being acquired;

any update to the AIC Plan is traceable to the preceding version and does not:

derogate from the commitments in the preceding version (unless otherwise agreed, in advance and in writing, by the Commonwealth Representative);

increase the risk of not satisfying the AIC Obligations; or

affect the Prescribed ACE Percentages unless supported by a Work Order change, as applicable.

Specific Content

AIC Program Summary

Note:  This section should be no more than three pages.  It should provide an introduction and summary of the strategic intent, objectives and commitments underpinning the AIC Plan consistent with the Australian industry policy requirements identified at clause 4.

The AIC Plan shall provide a summary of:

the Supplier’s strategy for achieving each of the AIC Objectives, including summarising the Supplier’s and AIC Subcontractors’ commitments over the Work Order Term for achieving these AIC Objectives;

any Sovereign Industrial Capability Priorities (SICPs) and Critical Industrial Capabilities applicable to the Work Order;

the Supplier’s and AIC Subcontractors’ commitments for delivering against the Australian Industry Activities (AIAs) , including Defence-Required Australian Industry Capabilities (DRAICs), applicable to the Work Order;

the overall Prescribed ACE Percentage for the Work Order;

the Supplier’s and AIC Subcontractors’ commitments over the Work Order Term to identifying and employing Local Businesses in the delivery of Work Order requirements;

the Supplier’s and AIC Subcontractors’ commitments over the Work Order Term to promote innovation and research and development (R&D) relating to the Work Order;

the Supplier’s commitments to supporting the Indigenous Procurement Policy in considering and involving recognised Indigenous enterprises within the Supplier’s supply chain and the employment of Indigenous Australians as part of delivering the Work Order requirements;

the Supplier’s commitments to engaging and employing veterans (previous members of the Australian Defence Force), including through suppliers that employ veterans, as part of delivering the Work Order requirements;

the Supplier’s and AIC Subcontractors’ commitments to Australian Industry over the longer term, particularly Small-to-Medium Enterprises (SMEs) and Local Businesses, including:

newly-proposed long-term partnering arrangements with particular Australian Entities;

enhancements to existing long-term partnering arrangements with particular Australian Entities; and

long-term engagement of particular Australian Entities in the Supplier’s supply chain; and

any other commitments or matters of note that demonstrate the Supplier’s commitment to achieving the AIC Objectives.

AIC Management

The AIC Plan shall:

identify the AIC manager, including name, title, contact details and the AIC manager’s responsibilities, position in the organisation, and describing the scope of influence of the AIC manager to promote and achieve the AIC Objectives;

describe the organisational arrangements for managing and undertaking the AIC program and related activities for the Work Order, including identifying the number of people involved in the AIC program and their respective responsibilities; and

where AIC Subcontractors are applicable to the Work Order, identifying the arrangements and division of responsibilities between the Supplier’s AIC organisation and each of the AIC Subcontractor’s AIC organisations.

The AIC Plan shall describe:

the assurance process used to ensure that the AIC Obligations are met and the AIC Objectives are being pursued, including:

the data to be captured, monitored and reported on;

the nature of, and the indicative frequency of, assurance activities; and

the reporting process for assurance activities planned / conducted during each reporting period;

the strategy and processes used to identify and remediate potential or actual problems with achieving the AIC Obligations, including:

describing the different processes to be employed when the problems are internal to the Supplier or are due to a Subcontractor, including the triggers that will be used to identify potential or actual problems;

the processes to be used to assist Australian Entities to address identified problems; and

the processes to be followed and associated timeframes to escalate the identified problems within the Supplier’s organisation, including to advise the Commonwealth of the problems; and

the details and outcomes that will be recorded in relevant Work Order progress reporting.

AIC Schedule

The AIC Plan shall include an AIC Schedule that details the activities being performed under the Work Order and over the Work Order Term in Australia or New Zealand and/or by each Australian Entity.

The AIC Schedule required by clause 5.2.3.1 shall be in the format defined in Annex B, or as otherwise agreed between the parties.

Creation, Enhancement and Maintenance of ANZ Industry Capabilities

Note:  This clause is intended to address all ANZ Industry Capabilities being created, enhanced or maintained under the Work Order.

The AIC Plan shall identify and describe the Supplier’s commitments and any AIC Subcontractors’ commitments under the Work Order in relation to the creation or enhancement of Industry Capabilities in Australia or New Zealand, including:

a brief description of each Industry Capability, including:

the identification (where known) of the Australian Entity(ies) in which the Industry Capability will be created or enhanced; and

the outcomes being sought in terms of:

the specific Industry Capabilities to be created or enhanced, including describing the specific creation of, or enhancements to, skills, knowledge, systems, technology and/or infrastructure;

Note:  The purpose of the following clause is to establish the exact boundaries of each Industry Capability.  For example, an Industry Capability established to undertake Software development may only undertake a portion of the overall work in relation to the Software to be delivered under the Work Order.

the utilisation of the newly-created or newly-enhanced Industry Capabilities by the Supplier and/or the AIC Subcontractors under the Work Order (ie, the specific work to be performed by the Industry Capability, and explicitly identifying any work that could be done by the Industry Capability, but is proposed to be undertaken by overseas companies); and

the potential opportunities that may exist more broadly to utilise the Industry Capabilities for future work with the Supplier and/or AIC Subcontractors, including for future work with Defence;

Note:  The purpose of the following clause is to obtain the Supplier’s and, if applicable, the AIC Subcontractors’ plans (ie, the who, what, when, where, how and why), as a series of commitments, for implementing each Industry Capability.

the specific initiatives and activities to be undertaken to achieve these outcomes, including:

where the Industry Capabilities will be established and/or evolved in Australian Entities other than the Supplier or AIC Subcontractors, the identification of the specific sectors of Australian Industry for which assistance will be provided, including, where known, the specific companies to receive the assistance (by company name and ACN/NZCN) and whether or not the company is an SME or a Local Business;

the specific commitments of the Supplier, AIC Subcontractor(s) and, where applicable, other companies (eg, a parent company or an Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) under a Subcontract) to achieve the identified outcomes, including in relation to the transfer of technology, know-how, know-why, and Technical Data and IP (TD/IP);

the timeframes and costs associated with these commitments, including stages of implementation, when any new or enhanced Industry Capabilities will be available, and whether or not the commitments involve the utilisation of government grants;

any assumptions, constraints and/or risks associated with the Industry Capability and/or the plan for its creation, enhancement or maintenance (as applicable), including the proposed mechanisms and/or activities to address or mitigate the identified assumptions, constraints and risks;

whether or not there are any specific Authorisations (eg, Export Approvals) required to enable the initiatives to be undertaken and, if so, the specific details and timings (which, for clarity, may be defined in relation to Milestones) associated with each different type of Authorisation;

whether or not these initiatives relate to skills requirements identified within the Defence skilling programs – either currently targeted or future gaps that have been identified – and, if so, the specific details in relation to each skill category being targeted;

any expectations of the Supplier with respect to the Commonwealth associated with the Industry Capability and/or the plan for its creation, enhancement or maintenance (as applicable); and

if a DRAIC Readiness Review (DRAICRR) is required under the Work Order, the proposed approach to satisfying the requirements for this review, including the organisations and individuals involved and their specific responsibilities, the proposed review venue, the detailed pre-requisites for conducting the review, actions to be undertaken during the review (eg, documentation to be reviewed), and the detailed review completion criteria.

how achievement of the specific enhancements and/or outcomes will be measured and reported; and

any planned follow-on activities or opportunities to ensure that the new or enhanced Industry Capabilities are maintained and will remain viable.

The AIC Plan shall identify and describe the Supplier’s commitments and any AIC Subcontractors’ commitments under the Work Order to support the maintenance of existing Industry Capabilities within Australian Industry, including:

the specific sectors of Australian Industry for which assistance will be provided or activities will be undertaken, including, where known, the specific companies to receive the assistance (by company name and ACN/NZCN) and whether or not the company is an SME or a Local Business; and

the specific activities and/or initiatives to be undertaken, including a brief description as to how these activities assist with the maintenance of existing Industry Capabilities in Australia or New Zealand.

Note:  The 2018 Defence Export Strategy sets out the Australian Government’s approach to achieving greater export success in order to build a stronger, more sustainable and globally competitive Australian Defence Industry.

The AIC Plan shall identify any commitments of the Supplier or AIC Subcontractors to promote the long-term sustainability of Industry Capabilities within Australian Industry, including identifying and describing:

any new Australian Industry company that has entered, or will be entering, the Supplier’s global supply chain as a result of the Work Order, including:

the nature of each company’s current or projected contribution; and

the estimated timeframe for each new company to enter the Supplier’s global supply chain; and

how Australian Industry has or will be provided with opportunities to enter export markets or facilitate domestic sales of goods or services as a result of the Work Order.

Supply Chain Assurance

The AIC Plan shall describe the Supplier’s plan, including the associated measures, to undertake assurance activities to monitor and assess the ANZ Sovereignty-related elements of their supply chain for the Services, as required by the SOW, including:

identifying the Subcontractors to be monitored;

describing the specific activities to be undertaken, including the methodologies to be employed and associated timeframes;

describing how AIC Subcontractors will be incorporated into these assurance activities; and

describing the mechanisms for reporting to the Commonwealth, including through the Work Order reporting requirements, the outcomes of the Supplier’s monitoring and assurance activities, including:

progress against the schedule of planned assurance activities;

a brief summary of the health of the implemented Industry Capabilities, including an assessment of each Subcontractor’s abilities to undertake the required sustainment activities; and

identification of any areas of concern, including the suggested plan to rectify, if required, any actions proposed to be undertaken by the Commonwealth, and a risk assessment if no action were to be taken to resolve the identified concerns.

Supplier and AIC Subcontractor Activities Contributing to ACE

Note:  The Supplier’s activities will be described in various Work Order plans.  The intent here is for the AIC Plan to set out the specific aspects of the Supplier’s and AIC Subcontractors’ activities that contribute to ACE, including:

all activities being conducted directly by the Supplier or an AIC Subcontractor, which are classified as ACE;

all activities being conducted by Subcontractors to the Supplier and AIC Subcontractors, which are classified as ACE; and

where the Supplier or an AIC Subcontractor is an overseas entity, all activities being conducted by the Supplier or AIC Subcontractor in support of ANZ Subcontractors, which are not classified as ACE, but enable ACE through the related ANZ Subcontract (eg, delivery of training and TD to transfer skills).

For those Supplier and AIC Subcontractor activities contributing to ACE (including Subcontractor activities), the AIC Plan shall provide a breakdown, which:

provides a breakdown that clearly describes the elements of the work being conducted in Australia or New Zealand (by postcode) and the elements being conducted overseas (by country), including separately identifying the activities being conducted by the Supplier from those being conducted by each AIC Subcontractor and cross-referring to the response provided to clause 5.2.6 where appropriate;

separately identifies the Supplier’s and each AIC Subcontractor’s activities in direct support of each Subcontractor activity being conducted in Australia or New Zealand (ie, identifying the specific activities pertaining to each Subcontractor), including:

identifying whether the Subcontractor is an SME or a Local Business; and

cross-referring to the response provided to clause 5.2.6, and supplemented by the identification of any other support being provided by the Supplier and/or each AIC Subcontractor to those Subcontractors, such as (for example) the provision of technical assistance, training, TD and equipment, and the secondment of Supplier Personnel;

if the delivery of Industry Capabilities as AIAs are applicable to the Work Order, identifies the specific AIAs to be created, enhanced or maintained by the Supplier (and/or a Subcontractor to the Supplier, including through an AIC Subcontractor), cross-referring to the response provided to clauses 5.2.6 where appropriate; and

separately identifies the Supplier’s and each AIC Subcontractor’s activities to foster and promote innovation and to undertake R&D in conjunction with Australian Industry, Defence Science and Technology Group (DSTG), Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation (CSIRO), state governments, academia and other Commonwealth agencies in support of the Commonwealth’s requirements under the Work Order.

For the set of Supplier and AIC Subcontractor activities contributing to ACE, the AIC Plan shall provide a summary of the Australian and/or New Zealand workforce profile for the Supplier and each AIC Subcontractor over the Work Order Term, as derived from the Staff / Skills Profile provided in the PMP and which identifies:

any increase or decrease in the total Australian and/or New Zealand workforce numbers for the Supplier and each AIC Subcontractor as an outcome of the Work Order, including by location (postcode); and

the areas impacted (eg, full-time vs part-time employees, apprenticeships and particular labour categories impacted), using the same breakdown of the workforce into the labour categories identified in the Staff / Skills Profile in the PMP.

Where the Supplier’s and each AIC Subcontractor’s activities contributing to ACE are facilitated or made possible due to technology transfer (or similar assistance) from another company (eg, from a parent company or from an OEM under a Subcontract), the AIC Plan shall describe the specific commitments of the other company(ies) to enable this work to be undertaken in Australia or New Zealand, including:

specific details in relation to transfer of technology, TD/IP, know-how and know-why; and

the mechanisms and timeframes associated with this assistance.

PUBLIC AIC PLAN

Notes:

The Public AIC Plan is designed to facilitate transparency and promote opportunities for Australian Industry to compete on merit throughout the period of the Work Order by publishing these opportunities on the Commonwealth’s internet website.

The initial Public AIC Plan is to be developed from the successful RFQ response and any negotiated changes prior to being submitted to the Commonwealth for Approval.

Updates to the Public AIC Plan are to be prepared, in accordance with the requirements of the Statement of Work and Work Order, to ensure that the plan accurately reflects forthcoming industry opportunities for publication on the Commonwealth internet website.

Generic Format and content

The Public AIC Plan shall be written on a company letterhead, signed by a duly authorised officer of the Supplier, and presented in a format that can be published on a Commonwealth internet website.

The Public AIC Plan shall only contain information of an appropriate security classification for publication on a public Internet website.

The Public AIC Plan shall, to the extent practicable, comply with the Australian Government Style Manual, which can be found at: https://www.stylemanual.gov.au/.

The Public AIC Plan shall include the information required under section 2, including using the same headings and structure.

Specific Content: Public AIC Plan

Company Details

The Public AIC Plan shall include the following company details:

company name;

address for the relevant company office; and

website details.

Executive Summary

The Public AIC Plan shall contain an executive summary that includes:

the Defence project number and the Work Order number;

an overview of the scope of the Work Order, including the Services to be provided and the scope of work to be performed by Australian Industry;

the Work Order Price and the overall Prescribed ACE Percentage for the Work Order;

the Work Order duration and forecast completion date; and

identification of the SICPs, Critical Industrial Capabilities, and DRAICs and other AIAs applicable to the Work Order.

This section of the Public AIC Plan shall be consistent with the “AIC Program Summary” required under clause 5.2.1.

Subcontracted Work

The Public AIC Plan shall describe the work to be subcontracted to Australian Industry, including:

a summary of the overall scope of work to be subcontracted;

the total forecast value of Subcontracts (in Australian dollars at Base Date), including those Subcontracts that have been signed;

ACE, as a percentage of the total forecast value of the Subcontracts;

for each AIC Subcontractor, the company name, location and nature of work that they have been contracted to perform; and

a list of Subcontractors within the supply chain that are an Indigenous Enterprise, a Small to Medium Enterprise, a Local Business and/or that have signed the Veterans employment commitment.

In addition to the information required by clause 2.3.1d, the Public AIC Plan should summarise any other notable Subcontracts (ie, other than Subcontracts with AIC Subcontractors) that demonstrate and promote the benefits of the Work Order in achieving the AIC Objectives.

Creation, Enhancement and Maintenance of Australian Industry Capabilities

The Public AIC Plan shall summarise the Supplier’s commitments, and any AIC Subcontractors’ commitments, to the creation of new Industry Capabilities, or the enhancement or maintenance of existing Industry Capabilities within Australia or New Zealand, including:

the sectors in Australian Industry for which assistance is being or will be provided, and the outcomes being sought from providing that assistance;

a brief summary of the specific work being conducted or the initiatives being implemented to assist with the creation, enhancement and/or maintenance of Industry Capabilities, particularly where these provide benefits to Defence, such as:

transfer of technology;

related AIAs, particularly DRAICs; and

training and skills development programs.

This section of the Public AIC Plan shall be consistent with the “Creation, Enhancement and Maintenance of ANZ Industry Capabilities” required under clause 5.2.6.

Future Work Opportunities

The Public AIC Plan shall summarise the future work opportunities available to Australian Industry, including:

a summary of the goods and services for which the source of supply is still to be determined and for which there are one or more opportunities for Australian Industry;

the nature of each opportunity, in terms of the scope of each proposed Subcontract for the goods and services identified in response to clause 2.5.1a; and

if applicable, the location(s) where it is required or desirable for the work to be performed (eg, if installation work is required to be performed on a Defence base).

This section of the Public AIC Plan shall be consistent with the “Opportunities for Australian Industry” required under clause 5.2.4.

Market Engagement

The Public AIC Plan shall describe the process for Australian Entities to apply for the work opportunities described in response to clause 2.5, including:

indicative timeframes for advertising, tendering and Subcontract award;

how the opportunity will be advertised (eg, through the use of supplier advocates, industry forums, professional networks, and the Supplier’s webpage); and

contact details for an appropriate representative from the Supplier to enable Australian companies to discuss the opportunities and register their interest.

This section of the Public AIC Plan shall be consistent with the “Contract Market Testing / Subcontract Establishment” required under clause 5.2.5.
