RECITALS

CIOG is responsible for ensuring that the Department of Defence has a dependable, secure and integrated ICT environment to support the Department of Defence's business and military operations.  CIOG's infrastructure is integral to core Department of Defence's functions, including intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance, communications, information warfare, logistics, command and management.

From time to time the Commonwealth may require the provision of certain ICT services to support CIOG's functions and activities and those of the Commonwealth more broadly.

The Commonwealth has established this standing offer ICT Provider Arrangement with a number of suppliers to provide certain ICT services.

The Supplier has agreed to provide the Services on the terms set out in this Deed and any Work Order formed under this Deed.

Deed Objectives

The objectives of the parties in entering into this Deed and each Work Order are:

to ensure that the Supplier provides the Services on time, on budget and to the required level of performance, safety, quality and capability, and otherwise in accordance with this Deed and each Work Order;

to assist the Commonwealth to establish a dependable, secure and integrated ICT environment to support the Commonwealth’s business and military operations;

to obtain value for money for the Commonwealth on a whole-of-life basis in relation to the Services in accordance with the Commonwealth Procurement Rules (CPRs), including through minimising Life Cycle Cost for the Services as required by each Work Order;

to have appropriate Intellectual Property rights for the acquisition, whole-of-life support and disposal of any Deliverables;

to facilitate the retention and enhancement of industry capabilities within Australia and New Zealand;

to encourage the most efficient use of resources;

to work within a framework that ensures the safety of persons and protection of the Environment, and complies with, and enables the Commonwealth to comply with all Laws, Commonwealth policies and any other relevant regulatory requirements;

to achieve these joint objectives through a culture of mutual respect and co-operation, and in an environment that fosters innovation, continuous improvement, cost efficiency and transparency;

to provide materiel elements and associated services, Intellectual Property (IP) rights, Technical Data (TD), know-how and know-why, which enable an effective capability that:

achieves the ADF’s mission(s) for the capability, as set out in the Work Order;

achieves Defence’s Sovereignty needs, including providing the Commonwealth with the capability to have enduring sovereign control over the operation and sustainment of its integrated ICT environment; and

ensures safety of persons, achieves Environmental Outcomes, and complies with, and enables the Commonwealth to comply with, all applicable environmental and safety laws and related regulatory requirements,

(collectively, the ADF Capability Objectives);

to:

within the context of clause 1.3(a)(iii), identify and pursue opportunities to maximise the participation of Australian Entities in the performance of this Deed and all Work Orders;

facilitate the creation, enhancement and/or maintenance of Industry Capabilities within Australia and New Zealand to satisfy the Work Order requirements, achieve the ADF Capability Objectives and support government’s and Defence’s goals for Australian Industry, as set out in the Defence industry policy; and

ensure that Australian Industry has the necessary technology, IP rights, TD, know-how and know-why to participate in future development and sustainment work in relation to the Services,

(collectively, the AIC Objectives); and

for each party to perform their respective obligations under this Deed and each Work Order with full commitment to fostering trust, cooperation and collaboration, including by:

communicating openly and honestly and in a timely manner;

sharing information to support effective decision making and using information shared for positive outcomes;

working together respectfully, productively and in a timely manner;

taking accountability and delivering on commitments;

assigning personnel with the requisite capability and capacity for collaborative working;

providing proactive, cooperative and flexible support when challenges arise; and

accepting and respecting decisions once made.

A Work Order may set out additional objectives to the Deed Objectives in respect of the Services to be provided under that Work Order (Work Order Objectives).  If there is any inconsistency between the Deed Objectives and any Work Order Objectives, the Deed Objectives prevail to the extent of the inconsistency.

Without in any way affecting or overriding the other terms of this Deed or a Work Order, each party must perform its obligations and enforce its rights under this Deed and each Work Order having regard to, and with the aim of, achieving the Objectives.

extend the date for providing a Quotation, before or after the date for providing the Quotation;

otherwise vary a RFQ, before or after the date for providing the Quotation; and/or

withdraw a RFQ, at any time before the Work Order is issued.

Evaluation of Quotations

Without limiting the Commonwealth’s other rights, the Commonwealth may:

conduct a financial investigation of a Quotation. The Supplier must cooperate with the Commonwealth in that regard and facilitate any such investigation;

exclude a Quotation from consideration or decline to proceed with a Quotation if it appears to the Commonwealth, acting reasonably, that any of the following apply:

the Quotation is incomplete or clearly non-competitive;

a representation or warranty given by the Supplier in its Quotation is false or misleading;

the Supplier fails to take a step required by the Commonwealth to eliminate or effectively manage a conflict of interest;

the Supplier, any of its Related Bodies Corporate, or any Officer of any of them has been convicted of bribery of a Commonwealth, State, Territory or foreign government official during the last 7 years;

The Commonwealth will evaluate a Quotation on the basis of:

best value for money consistent with Commonwealth procurement policies; and

any evaluation criteria listed in the RFQ.

When evaluating a Quotation, the Commonwealth may take into account additional information (whether that information is obtained through the RFQ process or by any other means) relevant to the Supplier’s Quotation.  Neither the lowest priced Quotation, nor any Quotation, will necessarily be Approved.

DISPUTES AND TERMINATION

Resolution of Disputes

General Principles

A party must not commence court proceedings relating to any Dispute unless that party has complied with the procedure for resolving Disputes set out in this clause 13.1.

The parties acknowledge and agree that the intent of this clause 13.1 is that the parties will seek to resolve Disputes in a non-adversarial manner, and at the lowest reasonably practicable level within their respective organisations.

The parties must use all reasonable endeavours to resolve Disputes in accordance with this clause 13.1 and as quickly as practicable.

Dispute Resolution Procedure

A party claiming a Dispute has arisen must promptly notify the other party setting out details of the Dispute.

The Commonwealth Contract Manager and the Supplier Contract Manager must, within 20 Working Days after a notice is given under clause 13.1(d) (or such other period as the parties may agree in writing), attempt to resolve the Dispute.

If the parties are unable to resolve a Dispute through the reasonable endeavours of the Commonwealth Contract Manager and the Supplier Contract Manager, the Dispute must then be referred to the Commonwealth Deed Representative and the Supplier Deed Representative.

If, despite using all reasonable endeavours, the Commonwealth Deed Representative and the Supplier Deed Representative are unable to resolve the Dispute within 20 Working Days (or such other period agreed by the parties in writing) after the referral under clause 13.1(f), either of the parties may, by notice to the other, refer the Dispute to mediation.

Mediation

The mediation of a Dispute must be held within 20 Working Days (or such other period as the parties may agree in writing) of a referral to mediation under clause 13.1(g).

The mediator of a Dispute will be agreed between the parties. If the parties cannot agree on a mediator within 5 Working Days after the referral of a Dispute to mediation under clause 13.1(g), the mediator will be chosen by the chairperson of the Resolution Institute or the chairperson of another resolution centre or institute determined by the Commonwealth.

The mediator’s role is to assist the parties to negotiate and resolve a Dispute. A mediator may not make a binding decision on a party to a Dispute unless the party otherwise agrees in writing.

Each party to a Dispute must pay its own costs of complying with this clause 13.1. The parties to a Dispute must also equally pay the costs of any mediator.

The parties must, despite any Dispute occurring, continue to perform their respective obligations under this Deed and the relevant Work Order.

Nothing in this clause 13.1 or the Governance Framework, prevents either party from seeking urgent interlocutory relief in relation to a Dispute.

In addition to any other rights it has in relation to this Deed and any Work Order, the Commonwealth may, at any time, for any reason and for its sole convenience, terminate this Deed or a Work Order (or reduce the scope of this Deed or a Work Order) in whole or in part by giving notice to the Supplier.

None of the other provisions of this Deed or any Work Order limit the Commonwealth's ability to terminate or reduce the scope of this Deed or any Work Order under this clause 13.4.

If this Deed or any Work Order is terminated or reduced under this clause 13.4, the Commonwealth's liability in respect of the termination or reduction is limited to:

payments for Services properly performed in accordance with the relevant Work Order before the date of termination or reduction takes effect; and

any reasonable additional costs incurred by the Supplier that are directly attributable to the termination or reduction provided that:

the Supplier has used its reasonable efforts to mitigate those costs; and

those costs have not already been included under paragraph (i) above,

and then only when the Supplier substantiates these amounts to the satisfaction of the Commonwealth. The Supplier is not entitled to profit calculated by reference to any period after the date the termination or reduction takes effect.

General Termination Provisions

If this Deed or any Work Order is terminated (in whole or in part) in accordance with clause 13 or otherwise:

the termination takes effect on:

the date of the notice of termination; or

if the notice of termination specifies a later date, the later date;

the Supplier must:

stop work in respect of any Work Order in accordance with the notice of termination;

comply with any directions given to the Supplier by the Commonwealth in relation to the termination (and the directions may be given in the notice of termination or at a later, reasonable time);

take all reasonable steps to mitigate all Loss in connection with the termination, including those arising from affected Subcontracts;

minimise any Loss resulting from the termination and protect Commonwealth Material and Intellectual Property of the Commonwealth;

deal with Commonwealth Material and Intellectual Property of the Commonwealth as reasonably directed by the Commonwealth;

return to the Commonwealth all GFM and the Commonwealth’s Confidential Information (except to the extent that the Supplier requires the GFM or the Commonwealth’s Confidential Information for work on Work Orders that continue in force); and

continue to work on any part of the Services not affected by the termination;

subject to clause 13.8, the parties will be relieved from future performance, without prejudice to:

any right, or cause of action, that has accrued at the date of termination; or

any amount owing under or in connection with this Deed or any Work Order as at the date of termination;

subject to clauses 10.8, 10.9 and 13.4, the right to recover damages, including full contractual damages, will not be affected;

the Supplier must, within 30 days after receipt of the notice of termination (or such other period agreed in writing by the parties), provide Deliverables to the Commonwealth (in their then current state of development) for the Services produced prior to the date of termination; and

the Supplier must deliver to the Commonwealth all Commonwealth Property that the Supplier or Supplier Personnel have in their possession in connection with this Deed or any Work Order.

The rights of the Commonwealth to terminate or reduce the scope of this Deed or any Work Order under clauses 13.2 and 13.4 are in addition to any other right or remedy the Commonwealth may have in relation to this Deed and any Work Order.

For clarity, the termination of this Deed or any Work Order (for any reason) does not prejudice or otherwise affect the Commonwealth’s rights under any Guarantee, including in relation to a call on a Deed of Guarantee and Indemnity or a drawdown of a Financial Security after the date of termination (as applicable).

General Provisions for Reductions in Scope

If the Commonwealth gives a notice under clause 13.4 to reduce the scope of this Deed or any Work Order, the Supplier must, in respect of the Services being removed from the scope of this Deed or any Work Order:

cease performing the removed scope on and from the date(s) specified in the notice; and

comply with clause 13.5 (which is to be modified as necessary to apply in relation to the removed scope).

If the Commonwealth gives a notice reducing the scope of this Deed or any Work Order:

the notice must:

include a DCP or WOCP (as applicable); or

include a direction to the Supplier under clause 11.1 or 11.2 (as applicable) to prepare and submit a DCP or WOCP;

to make amendments to this Deed or a Work Order (or both) that the Commonwealth determines, acting reasonably, are appropriate to give effect to the reduction in scope; and

the scope of this Deed or a Work Order (as the case requires) is reduced as specified in the notice on and from the day on which the notice takes effect.

Right of Commonwealth to Recover Money

Without limiting the Commonwealth’s other rights or remedies under this Deed, a Work Order or at Law, if the Supplier owes any debt to the Commonwealth in relation to this Deed or a Work Order, the Commonwealth may:

deduct the amount from payment of any Payment Claim; or

give the Supplier notice of the existence of a debt recoverable which must be paid by the Supplier within 30 days after receipt of notice.

The Commonwealth may exercise any or all of its rights in respect of any security provided in accordance with clauses 7.3 or 7.4 to recover any debt owing by the Supplier, except to the extent otherwise recovered by the Commonwealth under clause 13.7(a).

If the Commonwealth deducts the amount of a debt from any payment or security, it will notify the Supplier that it has done so.

If any sum of money owed to the Commonwealth is not received by its due date for payment, the Supplier must pay to the Commonwealth interest at the ATO sourced General Interest Charge rate current at the date the payment was due for each day the payment is late.

Where a provision under this Deed or a Work Order provides that the Commonwealth may recover from the Supplier under this clause 13.7 any cost or expense incurred by the Commonwealth, the cost or expense is deemed to be a debt due and payable by the Supplier to the Commonwealth.

Nothing in this clause 13.7 will affect the right of the Commonwealth to recover from the Supplier part of or the whole of any debt owed by the Supplier, or any balance that remains owing after deduction.

Survivorship

Any provision of this Deed or any Work Order which expressly or by implication from its nature is intended to survive the termination or expiration of this Deed or any Work Order, and any rights arising on termination or expiration will survive the termination or expiration of this Deed or any Work Order on its terms.

Without limiting clause 13.8(a), any provision dealing with Confidential Information, IP, Commonwealth Data, Defence security, privacy, and any warranties, guarantees, licences (other than any GFF Licence given under clause 3.9), indemnities, liability caps, rights to recover money or financial and performance securities given under this Deed and any Work Order will survive the termination or expiration of this Deed or any Work Order on its terms.

Unless otherwise directed by the Commonwealth in writing, the expiry or termination of this Deed will not affect the validity of any Work Orders between the parties which will each continue in accordance with their terms until their respective expiry or termination.
