Questions and Answers:

Question 1 : Can we request confirmation on why the FTE allocation across the 10
positions varies throughout the initial 18-month term and potential extension?
Answer 1: The FTE allocation across the 10 positions varies throughout the initial 18 -
month term and potential extension to meet key milestones and deliverables within
Defence.
Time has been allocated to achieve specific goals within certain periods, and to avoid
duplication of effort and unused work time.
Suppliers may provide a quotation with one person spread across multiple positions/
roles providing they are appropriately skilled.

Question 2: Are there incumbents currently in the 10 positions listed.
Answer 2: No

Question 3: Within the Services Provided table in the Schedule A – Statement of
Work in point 1.15 there are dates for the Business Analyst. One of the dates do not
align, please confirm the correct dates in yellow:
Business Analyst BUAN 4
 FY24/25: January 2025 – June 2025 0.94 FTE;
 FY25/26: June 2026 – June 2026 1.0 FTE; (should be June 2025 to June
2026 (question)).
 FY26/27: July 2026 – January 2027 1.0 FTE
Answer 3: It is correct  as written in 1.15 of the SOW for the portion of services
allocation that falls within FY25/26.
To clarify, a Business Analyst is required for one month in June 2026 at 1 FTE. The
requirement continues into FY26/27 so the role is required for a seven -month period
between June 2026 and January 2027 at 1 FTE.
There is no requirement for a Business Analyst between July 2025 and May 2026.

Question 4:  To meet the requirements, outlined in the Statement of Work, would
you consider higher rates than those noted on the SFIA v7 rate cards. I.e. utilising the
next rate SFIA code? Will candidates be discarded immediately if they are above?
Answer 4: Suppliers will not be immediately discarded if they quote above SFIA v7
rate cards. The Commonwealth will consider higher rates for per sonnel if  they
present value for money.

Question 5: You noted that Def ence Plaza, Sydney as the location preference, is this
where the majority of the team is sitting or is there provision for the team to be in
the other locations or remote (with access to DSN) noted in the Statement of Work
document?
Answer 5: Defence Plaza Sydney is the preferred work location for the personnel.
Per the amendment to 2.1 of the SOW , there is the option for personnel to work at
Defence Plaza Sydney or Campbell Park Offices as their primary work location.
Flexible work arrangements at other work locations listed in 2.1 of the SOW will be
considered on a case-by-case basis.
There are no remote working options. This is reflected in the addition of clause 2.2 of
the SOW.
