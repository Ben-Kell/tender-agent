PART 1 – SERVICES DESCRIPTION

Guidance note: There are 3 Modules in relation to Services which may be procured by the Commonwealth under this Deed and a Work Order. These are: 1) ICT Personnel Resources, 2) Application Services and 3) Systems Integration Services.

Part 1 of this Attachment A sets out the Services descriptions for each of the Modules.

Part 2 of this Attachment A sets out the Additional Module Terms, which includes some of the terms that may apply to each of Services (as they relate to the Modules).

ICT PERSONNEL RESOURCES

Services Description

ICT Personnel Resources includes the Supplier providing Supplier Personnel to:

deliver professional and technical ICT services to the Commonwealth; and/or

provide supplementary labour to the Commonwealth in the Commonwealth’s delivery of ICT services.

The Commonwealth may require Supplier Personnel to fill:

discrete operational and/or project roles to perform ICT services; or

roles designed to supplement Application Services and/or Systems Integration Services which are being performed by the Supplier.

ICT Personnel Resources must be set out in any Work Order by describing each procured role by reference to the required:

SFIA 7 skill set and level;

qualifications and any other skill sets;

Services to be delivered, including;

a Services summary;

any defined Deliverables;

any Milestones and the relevant Milestone Dates; and

any Performance Measures to be applied,

and by inclusion of any other requirements related to ICT Personnel Resources as specified in the Work Order.

APPLICATION SERVICES

Services Description

Application Services include Application Development Services and Application Maintenance Services.

Application Development Services may include some or all of the following types of Services, as set out in the Work Order:

preparation of the delivery strategy, key business drivers and organisational capabilities for new Applications;

whole of solution planning (including lifecycle planning) for new Applications;

in relation to new Applications (and any Major Enhancements):

business needs analysis and requirements development, including technical requirements;

solution architecture design and development;

design and design review;

build;

Testing;

user acceptance testing;

transition and deployment into production; and

verification and validation (pre-production and post-production);

supporting implementation of related and/or ancillary Services, including Systems Integration Services, provision of Hardware, Cloud Services, Managed Services, Retained Data migration or any other Services, as required;

project management, project governance, project administration, Incident and Problem reporting and resolution, reporting and training Services, to support the activities listed in the subparagraphs above, as required;

procurement and/or provision of any business, functional and/or Technical Data documentation to support the Services listed in the subparagraphs above, as required; and

any other Services related to Application Development Services, as specified in the Work Order.

Application Maintenance Services include some or all of the following types of Services, as set out in the Work Order:

design Services, which include all activities that are required to deliver a framework for Application Maintenance Services (and any other Maintenance Services that may be required for Integrated Systems and/or Existing Systems), including whole of solution Application life cycle management;

operational Services, which include all activities required for end-to-end delivery of ITIL® aligned Application Maintenance Services (and any other Maintenance Services that may be required for Integrated Systems and Existing Systems), including but not limited to:

service catalogue management;

service capacity management;

service availability and continuity management;

system security management;

service operation, including Service Level management;

Incident and Problem management;

data management; and

customer engagement and feedback to support continuous improvement;

in relation to Minor Enhancements:

business needs analysis and requirements development, including technical requirements;

design and design review;

build;

Testing;

user acceptance testing;

transition and deployment into production;  and

verification and validation (pre-production and post-production);

supporting implementation of related and/or ancillary Services including Systems Integration Services, provision of Hardware, Cloud Services, Managed Services, Retained Data migration or any other Services, as required;

project management, project governance, project administration, Incident and Problem reporting and resolution, reporting and training Services to support the activities listed in the subparagraphs above, as required;

procurement and/or provision of any business, functional and/or Technical Data documentation to support the Services listed in the subparagraphs above, as required; and

any other Services related to Application Maintenance Services as specified in the Work Order.

SYSTEMS INTEGRATION SERVICES

Services Description

Systems Integration Services includes the:

connection and aggregation of disparate subsystems, functionalities or Software to form one coordinated and functional Integrated System; and

developing, implementing and monitoring networks, connections and communications between subsystems and systems, or between subsystems, systems and Existing Systems.

Systems Integration Services includes some or all of the following Services, as set out in the Work Order:

engineering requirements analysis, development and validation;

functional specifications development;

design and design review;

build;

Testing;

user acceptance testing;

Retained Data migration;

transition and deployment into production;

verification and validation (pre-production and post-production);

supporting implementation of related and/or ancillary Services including  Application Services, provision of Hardware, Cloud Services, Managed Services or any other Services, as required;

project management, project governance, project administration, Incident and Problem reporting and resolution, reporting and training Services, to support the activities listed in the subparagraphs above, as required;

procurement and/or provision of any business, functional and/or Technical Data documentation to support the Services listed in the subparagraphs above, as required; and

any other Services related to Systems Integration Services as specified in the Work Order.

ANCILLARY SERVICES

Services Description

In addition to the Services described in clauses 1 to 3 above, the Deed makes provision for the following types of Services to be procured under a Work Order:

Hardware;

Cloud Services; and

Managed Services.

For clarity, as set out in clause 1.10(d) of the COD, it is intended that a Work Order will only include:

Hardware, Cloud Services and/or the provision of Software as an ancillary Service to the provision of Application Services or Systems Integration Services (or both); and

Managed Services as a service under the provision of either Application Services, Systems Integration Services and/or ICT Personnel Resources Services.

Part 2 of this Attachment A sets out the Additional Module Terms that may be incorporated into a Work Order for the provision of Hardware, Cloud Services and/or Managed Services.

Part 2 of this Attachment A also includes Additional Module Terms (including in relation to Software and Sustainment) that may be incorporated into a Work Order for other Services procured under a Module.

PART 2 – ADDITIONAL MODULE TERMS

APPLICATION OF THE ADDITIONAL MODULE TERMS

Introduction

This Part 2 of Attachment A sets out Additional Module Terms for the performance of Services under this Deed.

Some of the Additional Module Terms will apply automatically where services that are in the nature of the Services covered by the relevant term are included in a Work Order.

Some of the Additional Module Terms will only apply to the extent that:

a Work Order states that the particular term will apply; and/or

the relevant terms are expressly included in a Work Order.

Each clause within this Part 2 of Attachment A indicates whether the Additional Module Terms will apply unless excluded or will only apply where included in a Work Order.  For clarity, a Work Order may expressly amend, including by removal, the terms of this Attachment A.

ICT PERSONNEL RESOURCES

General

This clause 2 sets out additional provisions that, unless excluded in a Work Order, apply to all Services described in a Work Order as (or that are in the nature of) ICT Personnel Resources.

The Supplier must:

ensure that all Supplier Personnel have the appropriate skills (including under the SFIA 7 framework), qualifications, Authorisations and experience to provide the Services in accordance with the requirements of this Deed and the relevant Work Order; and

if required by the Commonwealth, provide all relevant documentation (including curriculum vitae) to demonstrate that the Supplier Personnel have the appropriate skills qualifications, Authorisations and experience to provide the Services.

APPLICATION SERVICES

General

This clause 3 sets out the additional provisions that, unless excluded in a Work Order, apply to all Services described in a Work Order as (or that are in the nature of) Application Services.

Provision of Application Services

The Supplier must ensure that:

it communicates and coordinates with the Commonwealth, Other Suppliers and the Commonwealth’s end-users of the relevant Application in providing Application Services;

all Application Services are properly integrated with the production scheduling for the Application Services, in accordance with any applicable Approved Project Management Plan;

it provides all Application Services, including the transition of an Application into a production environment (if applicable), in a way that ensures minimum disruption to the Commonwealth, Other Suppliers and the Commonwealth’s end-users of the Application;

the Application Services are deployed in a controlled, tested, traceable and structured manner and in a way that ensures that all changes to an Application (including Major Enhancements and Minor Enhancements) can be promptly reported to the Commonwealth; and

the Application Services are provided in a way that prevents Incidents or Problems from occurring in relation to the Application Services.

SYSTEMS INTEGRATION SERVICES

General

This clause 4 sets out the additional provisions that apply to all Services that are specified in a Work Order as (or that are in the nature of) Systems Integration Services.

Integrated Systems

Unless otherwise specified in a Work Order, where a Work Order requires the Supplier to supply an Integrated System, the Supplier must ensure that:

all components of the Integrated System will interact and work together and meet the specifications referred to in the relevant Work Order; and

the Integrated System as installed will be properly integrated and functional with all relevant Existing Systems.

Data Migration

Unless otherwise specified in a Work Order, where a Work Order requires the Supplier to migrate data from an Existing System onto an Integrated System (Retained Data), the Supplier must extract, transform, cleanse, enhance, enrich and upload the Retained Data onto the Integrated System.

Without limiting the Supplier’s obligations under clause 11.15 of the COD, the Supplier must ensure that any data migration or transfer (including of any Commonwealth Data) carried out by the Supplier or a Subcontractor under or in connection with the Work Order does not result in:

data loss;

data corruption; or

any reduction in the accessibility or useability of the data.

HARDWARE

General

This clause 5 sets out additional provisions that, unless excluded in a Work Order, apply in connection with the provision of Hardware and associated maintenance and support under this Deed and a Work Order.

The provisions of this clause 5 apply to the supply of all Hardware under this Deed and a Work Order except to the extent expressly stated otherwise in the Work Order.

This clause 5 does not apply in relation to the leasing of Hardware by the Supplier.

The provision of Services in relation to Hardware may include:

the supply of Hardware;

the installation and integration of Hardware;

Preventative Maintenance;

Corrective Maintenance; and/or

technical support services for Hardware.

The Supplier must grant, or must ensure the grant of, a Licence to the Commonwealth in accordance with clause 8 (Additional Software Provisions) to all Software that is installed or incorporated in the Hardware.

The Supplier must ensure that all Hardware provided as a Deliverable is:

new (and not refurbished); and

produced or authorised by the OEM,

unless otherwise specified in a Work Order.

Spare Parts and Support Equipment

The Supplier must, for 5 years after ownership of Hardware passes to the Commonwealth in accordance with clause 6.13 of the COD (or such other timeframe specified in a Work Order), maintain facilities or other arrangements to allow the Commonwealth to procure sufficient quantities of new (and not refurbished) spare parts (that are produced or authorised by the OEM) and support equipment so that the Hardware may be properly maintained.

If, during the period described in clause 5.2(a), the Supplier becomes aware that its ability to supply spare parts and support equipment to the Commonwealth may be adversely affected, it must:

notify the Commonwealth immediately of that event; and

if there will be a final production run of spare parts and support equipment, nominate in the notice the date by which the Commonwealth may place final orders.

The Supplier must ensure that provisions corresponding to clauses 5.2(a) and 5.2(b) are included in all Approved Subcontracts under which spare parts or support equipment may be provided.

The Commonwealth is not bound to order any, or any particular quantity of, spare parts or support equipment from the Supplier.

Preventative Maintenance

If a Work Order includes Preventive Maintenance for specified Hardware, the Supplier must:

submit and achieve Approval or Acceptance (as applicable), within the period specified in the Work Order, of a schedule for performing the Preventive Maintenance; and

perform the Preventive Maintenance at the times set out in the Approved or Accepted (as applicable) schedule.

The schedule must ensure that Preventive Maintenance is performed:

as often as needed to ensure that the Hardware:

meets the technical requirements of the Commonwealth, including as to availability, as specified in a Work Order on a continuous basis; and

is and remains fit for the purposes for which it is used by the Commonwealth; and

at times when the Hardware is not being used by the Commonwealth or, if that is not practicable, at times when the Preventive Maintenance is likely to cause the least practicable disruption to the Commonwealth.

In performing Preventive Maintenance, the Supplier must ensure that the disruption to the Commonwealth caused by the Preventive Maintenance is minimised.

The Commonwealth must provide access to the Hardware reasonably necessary to enable the Supplier to provide Preventative Maintenance in accordance with the Work Order and the Approved or Accepted (as applicable) schedule.

The Supplier must keep detailed and accurate records of all Preventative Maintenance performed, and the records must be in an Approved data format and stored in a system that is accessible by the Commonwealth.

Corrective Maintenance

If a Work Order includes Corrective Maintenance for specified Hardware, the Supplier must, after being notified by the Commonwealth of a problem, issue or failure with such Hardware, promptly carry out Corrective Maintenance and restore the Hardware such that it:

meets the technical requirements of the Commonwealth, including as to availability, as specified in a Work Order on a continuous basis; and

is fit for the purposes for which it is used by the Commonwealth.

The Supplier must when performing Corrective Maintenance:

replace or repair parts as necessary;

implement measures to minimise disruption to the Commonwealth’s operations during the performance of the Corrective Maintenance;

perform the Corrective Maintenance at such times likely to cause the least possible disruption to the Commonwealth, and in all cases only by prior Approval; and

comply with any operating requirements in relation to the Corrective Maintenance notified to the Supplier by the Commonwealth from time to time.

The Commonwealth must provide access to the Hardware as reasonably necessary to enable the Supplier to provide Corrective Maintenance at the times Approved and in accordance with the relevant Work Order.

Where and to the extent that the Commonwealth determines that Corrective Maintenance is required to be performed, or has been performed, by the Supplier during the Defect Notification Period to remedy a Defect in the Hardware, the Supplier will bear all rectification costs in accordance with clause 8.3 of the COD.

Help Desk

If a Work Order includes Help Desk support, the Supplier must, for the period specified in the relevant Work Order, provide Help Desk support in accordance with this clause 5.5 and the relevant Work Order.

The Supplier must ensure that all Supplier Personnel that provide Help Desk support:

have the requisite skills and technical experience to provide remote troubleshooting and assistance to Commonwealth users; and

use all reasonable endeavours to provide the Commonwealth users with remote support to resolve any issue, Incident or Failure that has been notified to the Help Desk.

A Work Order may specify that the Help Desk must provide different levels of support to the Commonwealth users’ tickets based on their priority.

CLOUD SERVICES

General

This clause 6 sets out additional provisions that, unless excluded in a Work Order, apply in connection with the provision of Cloud Services under this Deed and a Work Order.

The provisions of this clause 6 will apply to the provision of all Cloud Services under this Deed and a Work Order except to the extent expressly stated otherwise in the Work Order.

Cloud Services may include:

Software-as-a-Service (SaaS);

Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS); and/or

Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS).

Continuous Modification

The Supplier may, from time to time, make technical or product enhancements, modifications, updates or legal changes to the Cloud Services (each a Continuous Modification).

Where the Supplier proposes to make a Continuous Modification, the Supplier must, at least 90 days prior to the proposed Change Effective Date, notify the Commonwealth of the matter and provide details of:

the type and nature of the Continuous Modification; and

any effects that the Continuous Modification may have on the performance of the Cloud Services.

The Commonwealth may, within 20 Working Days after receipt of the Supplier's notice in accordance with clause 6.2(b), notify the Supplier that it either Approves or does not Approve the Continuous Modification.  If the Commonwealth does not Approve the Continuous Modification, the notice must give reasons for not Approving.

If the Commonwealth does not give the Supplier notice in accordance with clause 6.2(c), the Continuous Modification will automatically take effect on the proposed Change Effective Date.

If, after a Continuous Modification has been implemented by the Supplier, the Commonwealth determines that the Continuous Modification:

has materially diminished or reduced the functionality of the Cloud Services; and/or

makes the Cloud Services unfit for purpose,

the Commonwealth may:

give the Supplier a Default Notice under clause 13.3 of the COD; and

if the Supplier fails to remedy the Default within the period specified in the Default Notice, terminate the relevant Work Order or reduce the scope of the Cloud Services.

If the Commonwealth terminates the relevant Work Order or reduces the scope of the Cloud Services, the Supplier must refund to the Commonwealth any amounts paid in advance to the Supplier for any Cloud Services that would have otherwise been provided to the Commonwealth after the termination date or the date that the scope is reduced.

Disengagement and Media Decommissioning

Without limiting any obligations for Phase-Out under the relevant Work Order (including under any Phase-Out Plan), the Supplier must take all actions that are reasonably necessary to ensure there is an efficient and effective disengagement from the Cloud Services and/or transfer to the Commonwealth or its nominee when the provision of Cloud Services ends under a Work Order, so as to cause minimum disruption and inconvenience to the Commonwealth.

Without limiting the generality of clause 6.3(a), the Supplier must:

liaise with the Commonwealth to plan and implement all necessary actions to facilitate the disengagement from the Cloud Services and/or transfer to the Commonwealth or its nominee;

comply with its security and data protection obligations under this Deed and the relevant Work Order;

transfer to the Commonwealth or its nominee all Commonwealth Data in a format that is readily usable (without requiring an additional licence) by the Commonwealth or its nominee and provide sufficient information to allow reading of, searching of, interpretation and processing of the Commonwealth Data by the Commonwealth or its nominee;

remove all backed-up versions of Commonwealth Data stored on all infrastructure or platforms provided as part of the Cloud Services; and

not destroy any Commonwealth Data unless and to the extent the destruction was Approved.

If required, the Supplier must provide the Commonwealth with information that the Commonwealth or its nominee reasonably requires, to verify the Supplier's performance of its obligations under this clause 6.3.

Minimum Usage Rights

The Supplier grants to the Commonwealth for the relevant Work Order Term, in respect of the Cloud Services, an irrevocable right to:

use and access the Cloud Services:

from the Commonwealth’s environment or system; and

by any end user (including Commonwealth Personnel, Other Suppliers and Commonwealth Agencies) authorised by the Commonwealth;

permit adaptation and configuration of the Cloud Services to the extent necessary to enable them to be used on the Commonwealth's environment or system; and

test, evaluate and confirm the suitability and compliance with Service Levels (or any other relevant Performance Measures) in relation to the Cloud Services.

The rights in this clause 6.4 apply to the Commonwealth’s (and its end users') use of the Cloud Services to the exclusion of any terms or conditions of use, restrictions or additional charges, including as set out in any:

licensing or usage statement;

shrink wrap arrangements;

hyperlink terms; or

click-wrap arrangements,

wherever appearing, and whether or not user 'acceptance' is required or provided.

IRAP Assessment

Without limiting the Supplier’s other obligations under this Deed or a Work Order, including in relation to security, the parties acknowledge and agree that:

in accordance with the Cloud Security Guidance, the Commonwealth requires an Information Security Registered Assessors Program (IRAP) assessment report of the Cloud Services; and

prior to Approving and/or Accepting the Cloud Services, the Commonwealth will consider the IRAP assessment report to determine whether the Cloud Services meets the Commonwealth’s security requirements.

The Supplier must provide reasonable assistance to the Commonwealth (and the Commonwealth’s nominated IRAP assessor) in relation to any IRAP assessment, including the preparation of the relevant report.

The IRAP assessment will be conducted in accordance with:

the Cloud Security Guidance, including to be substantially in the form of the Cloud Security Assessment Report Template and the cloud security controls matrix referred to in the Cloud Security Guidance;

the Information Security Manual; and

the DSPF (as applicable) and any other relevant Commonwealth security policies.

In addition to the high level criteria identified in the Cloud Security Guidance, the Commonwealth may require that the IRAP assessment report includes additional or more detailed criteria in respect to the assessment of the Cloud Services.

The Supplier must implement each IRAP assessment report recommendation that is identified in a notice from the Commonwealth to the Supplier within 3 months of receipt of the notice, or as otherwise agreed between parties.

The Supplier acknowledges that additional IRAP assessments may be required throughout the Work Order Term and agrees that the Cloud Services are to be reassessed and that a further IRAP assessment report is prepared:

at the times specified in the relevant Work Order;

before there is any changes made to the scope of the Cloud Services, and to the extent that such changes were not assessed in the previous IRAP assessment report; and

without limiting clause 6.2, each time the Supplier and/or the Cloud Services provider makes significant changes to the Cloud Services or any other Services that impact the security documented in the IRAP assessment report.

Clauses 6.5(b) to 6.5(e) also apply to IRAP reassessments.

The Supplier agrees to notify the Commonwealth of:

without limiting clause 6.2, any changes to the Cloud Services or other Services that impact the security documented in the IRAP reassessment report; and

any other security events that may impact the security and risk of the Commonwealth’s environment or system or Commonwealth Data.

MANAGED SERVICES

General

This clause 7 sets out the additional provisions that, unless excluded in a Work Order, apply to Services described in a Work Order that are in the nature of Managed Services.

Managed Services must be provided in accordance with the COD and any further Special Terms and Conditions for the performance of the Managed Services that may be set out in the relevant Work Order.

Unless and to the extent otherwise specified in the Work Order, clauses 7.2 to 7.5 will also apply to Third Party Contracts with relevant service providers in connection with the provision of Managed Services.

Third Party Contracts

Where required by the Commonwealth, the Supplier must (whether in connection with the procurement of Managed Services or other Services):

divide the relevant project into appropriate work packages for Third Party Contracts in order to procure the execution and completion of the project in an economic and efficient manner; and

assist the Commonwealth in preparing, tendering and negotiating each Third Party Contract, including ensuring that each scope of service is compatible with the relevant work packages without unnecessary or avoidable duplication of resources or costs.

The Supplier must ensure that all tasks, activities and processes for the procurement of items or services in relation to Third Party Contracts (whether in connection with the procurement of Managed Services or other Services) are conducted:

consistently with the principles of the CPRs, including the rules in relation to “encouraging competition”, “efficient, effective, economical and ethical procurement”, “accountability and transparency” and the need to achieve “value for money”, as described in the CPRs;

on terms which achieve value for money outcomes for the Commonwealth;

to the highest standards of probity, fairness and equal opportunity; and

in accordance with all Laws and Commonwealth policies.

The Supplier must, where the Commonwealth gives notice to the Supplier, enter into a Third Party Contract:

as principal (in which case the agreement will be a Subcontract and clause 11.11 of the COD will apply to that agreement); or

as agent for the Commonwealth (in which case the agreement will be a Managed Service Subcontract and clauses 7.3 to 7.5 will apply to that agreement).

Managed Service Subcontracts

The Supplier must obtain Approval before signing a Managed Service Subcontract (and such Approval may be withheld or may be given subject to the Approval of the terms of the proposed Managed Service Subcontract).

The Supplier must manage each Managed Service Subcontractor’s performance in accordance with the Managed Service Subcontract.  Unless otherwise set out in this Deed or the relevant Work Order, the Supplier’s Managed Services include:

monitoring the performance of the Managed Service Subcontractor;

using reasonable endeavours to ensure (through management and monitoring) that the Managed Service Subcontractor performs the services in accordance with the relevant Managed Service Subcontract;

assessing the quality and compliance of the services and materials produced under the Managed Service Subcontract with the Managed Service Subcontractor’s obligations under the Managed Service Subcontract, and reporting to the Commonwealth the results of that assessment;

promptly advising the Commonwealth of any problems, issues or failure of the Managed Service Subcontractor in respect of the Managed Service Subcontract of which the Supplier is aware;

advising the Commonwealth of any steps required to be taken to avoid or mitigate any material adverse effects in relation to any poor performance of the services under the Managed Service Subcontract;

providing to the Commonwealth any other information as the Commonwealth may from time to time reasonably require in relation to the performance of the services by the Managed Service Subcontractor; and

escalating to the Commonwealth for resolution any issues that the Supplier has not been able to resolve with the Managed Service Subcontractor itself, after using all reasonable endeavours.

Without limiting the Supplier’s fiduciary duties, the Supplier must:

act in the Commonwealth’s best interests in relation to Managed Service Subcontracts, including when negotiating terms and conditions, entering into the agreements, and managing the agreements;

act in accordance with the powers and duties (or obligations) of the Commonwealth under the Managed Service Subcontracts and such that no deliberate, knowing or negligent act or omission of the Supplier would place the Commonwealth in breach of the Managed Service Subcontracts; and

act at all times within the scope of its authority as the Commonwealth’s agent, including to act in accordance with any Commonwealth direction given from time to time.

The Supplier must, promptly after receiving notice from the Commonwealth, comply with any request by the Commonwealth to terminate, assign, transfer or novate to the Commonwealth (or a third party nominated by the Commonwealth) any Managed Service Subcontract.

Co-operation with Managed Service Subcontractors

The Commonwealth will ensure that each Managed Service Subcontractor is required to provide all necessary co-operation with the Supplier to enable the Supplier to perform the Managed Services.

Managed Service Subcontract Invoices

The Supplier must verify the accuracy of all invoices from each Managed Service Subcontractor, and confirm to the Commonwealth whether the invoice is correct and payable.  Any dispute or discrepancy in any Tax Invoice must be raised by the Supplier with the Managed Service Subcontractor first.  The Commonwealth will pay all valid Tax Invoices directly to the Managed Service Subcontractor.

ADDITIONAL SOFTWARE PROVISIONS

General

This clause 8 sets out additional provisions that, unless excluded in a Work Order, apply in connection with the provision of any Software under this Deed and a Work Order (including Software which is licensed by the Supplier or a third party).

The provisions of this clause 8 apply to the provision of all Software under this Deed and a Work Order, except to the extent expressly stated otherwise in this clause 8 or the Work Order.

The Supplier must:

provide the Software via the delivery mechanism (which may be through means of an electronic download) specified in the Work Order (or if none is specified, as notified by the Commonwealth) and install, configure and integrate the Software in accordance with the relevant Work Order and the requirements of the Commonwealth;

supply documentation as necessary to enable the Commonwealth to exercise its rights under this Deed or the relevant Work Order; and

ensure that the Software, when installed and implemented, provides the functions and meets the performance, purpose and other requirements specified in the relevant Work Order (including for the duration of the Defect Notification Period and for the term of any Sustainment Services).

Supplier Licenced Software

Unless otherwise specified in a Work Order (including in respect of Software provided as COTS), the Supplier grants, or must ensure a grant of, a Licence to the Commonwealth to install, use, operate, copy, support, maintain and modify (collectively “use”) the Software.

Without limitation, the Licence granted under clause 8.2(a) entitles the Commonwealth to:

make as many copies (including back-up copies) of the Software as may be reasonably necessary for the Commonwealth’s internal business purposes;

authorise any Commonwealth Personnel or any Commonwealth Agency to use the Software for the Commonwealth’s internal business purposes;

unless otherwise specified in a Work Order, authorise any third party to use the Software for the purposes of providing services to the Commonwealth; and

if the Software is intended to be accessed and used by external users, authorise such users to use the Software for the purposes described in a Work Order, or for the purposes for which the Supplier knows or ought reasonably to know the Software will be used.

Third Party Licensed Software

If the Commonwealth requires third party licensed Software to be provided under a Work Order, the Work Order may specify that:

the Supplier must enter into a Third Party Contract as principal (in which case the agreement will be a Subcontract) and the agreement must include:

Where Supplier Personnel perform Services for a portion of a Working Day, a pro rata calculation of the relevant daily Labour Rate will be used. The pro rata daily Labour Rate will be calculated based on the relevant daily Labour Rate, divided by 8 hours multiplied by the number of hours the Supplier Personnel performs the Services.  For example, the amount payable for 6 hours worked in a Working Day would be calculated as follows:

(daily Labour Rate / total hours in a Working Day) x actual hours worked in the relevant Working Day. Where:

daily Labour Rate e.g.: $1,750.00

total hours in a Working Day: 8 hours

actual hours worked in the Working Day: 6 hours

($1,750.00 / 8 hours) x 6 hours = $1,312.50
